FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Here are some frequently asked questions (FAQs) along with their answers:


Q1. Why study with us?
-Studying with us offers a dynamic learning environment where cutting-edge research intersects with hands-on practical experience in the rapidly evolving field of computer science. Our distinguished faculty, with diverse expertise spanning various specializations, is dedicated to fostering a supportive and collaborative community. Additionally, our strong industry connections provide unique opportunities for internships, networking, and career advancement, ensuring that our graduates are well-equipped for success in the technology landscape.

Q2. What is our approach?
-Our approach integrates a balanced curriculum that combines foundational principles with the latest advancements in computer science. Emphasizing a hands-on learning experience, our students engage in practical projects and collaborate with faculty on cutting-edge research. We foster a collaborative and inclusive learning environment, encouraging critical thinking and problem-solving skills essential for success in the dynamic field of computer science.

Q3: What are the admission requirements for the Computer Science program?
- You must pass the level 100 Mathematics courses and DCIT courses to gain placement into the department.

Q4: How can I apply for scholarships or financial aid within the Computer Department?
-Information about scholarships and financial aid opportunities can only be found on the university's website. We encourage you to explore various options such as grants, and external funding sources. The application process and deadlines are detailed on the website.

Q5. What research opportunities are available for graduate students in the Computer Department?
-The Computer Department offers a range of research opportunities in areas such as artificial intelligence, machine learning, cybersecurity, and more. Students can collaborate with faculty on ongoing projects or pursue their research initiatives. Visit the Research page for details on current projects and faculty interests.

Q6. How can I get involved in extracurricular activities related to computer science?
Joining student clubs, attending hackathons, and participating in coding competitions are excellent ways to engage in extracurricular activities. Check out our Student Life section for information on clubs and events within the Computer Department.

Q7. Is there a career services center to assist computer science students in finding internships and job opportunities?
- Yes, our Career Services Center provides dedicated support for computer science students, offering resources such as resume workshops, career fairs, and networking events. Visit the Career Services page on the school's website for details on upcoming events and job placement assistance.
